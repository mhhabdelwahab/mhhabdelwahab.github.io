Research Focus

My research focuses on pushing the boundaries of robotics systems for real-world applications, with an emphasis on computer vision, robot perception & navigation, and autonomous inspection systems.
My work can be broadly categorized into two key areas: Robotic Perception and Navigation and Autonomous Inspection Systems.

Robotic Perception and Navigation

In the realm of robotic perception, we develop advanced techniques for autonomous navigation and environment perception. We focus on visual SLAM technologies that can adapt to diverse environments, such as indoor settings and underwater terrains.

  • Bio-inspired visual perception systems for enhanced robot sensing;

  • SLAM algorithms integrated with BIM for accurate indoor navigation;

  • 3D-Lidar and vision-based localization for industrial applications;

  • Active perception based human detection for service robots.


Autonomous inspection systems

The autonomous inspection systems are designed for remote and unmanned environments such as marine environments and industrial plants. By incorporating deep learning algorithms and advanced sensors, these systems are capable of detecting anomalies and ensuring operational safety.

  • Design and development of AI-powered autonomous inspection robots;

  • Real-time equipment monitoring and anomaly detection using advanced computer vision techniques.
