Biography

    I am the Marine Robotics Research Lead at Khalifa University Robotics Research Center, specializing in computer vision and robotic navigation. My research spans areas such as Visual SLAM, Robot Perception, and the development of intelligent autonomous systems for industrial applications. I hold a PhD in Mechatronics and Robotics Engineering from Osaka University (Japan) and EJUST (Egypt), and my work in robotics has been applied across various industries, including autonomous inspection robots and healthcare robotics.

    I am a passionate educator, having taught computer vision and artificial intelligence courses at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Additionally, I am a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA) in the UK and actively engage in mentoring and collaboration on robotics and AI projects.
